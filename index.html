Jake's Top Five Tips for Email Deliverability

Ensure your email deliverability is optimised for success. Discover how these simple tips can turn your emails from e-waste to e-treasure, from maintaining good list hygiene, to desciphering the secret conversations your email server is having behind your back. Learn how to beat the email police and turn email into your most rewarding channel.
Jake Harry Published: October 29, 2024

Contents
  • The importance of a win-back programs
  • Timing considerations for win-back campaigns
  • Email content in win-back campaigns
  • Create successful win-back automation campaigns today
Share

Mastering the art of e-mail deliverability can seem like painting the roof of the sistene chapel. Turn the odds in your favour and start painting like Michaelangelo.

In this blog post, we will explore five key areas of e-mail deliverability that focus on identifying issues which may be preventing your emails from having ultimate success, and sharing some tips and tricks to ensure that you are staying on top of email deliverability.

The importance of list hygiene

Hygiene? Cleaning supplies? You might be familiar with bleach, vinegar and elbow-grease. But have you heard of data entry issues, preference centres and unengaged contacts?

Dear N/A,

We hate to see improperly addressed emails, but how can we be sure that we're addressing our customers as they would like to be addressed, and how can we be sure that we have accurate information saved for our contacts?

Ask and Confirm

When collecting information from our customers, whether on sign up, or through follow-up campaigns, we should always use a double-opt in approach, and give our customers a chance to review any information that they have entered with us. After all, we're only human, right? And humans make mistakes. By confirming our customers details and preferences we can ensure that we are communicating with them on their terms, and addressing them as they wish to be addressed. When using customer information that is attained through other sources, proceed with caution and make sure that it's both information you can trust, and you have permission to use the information from the customer.

Two Years since you WHAT?

Emailing customers that have not engaged with you recently is a recipe for disaster. Not only are you diluting the key metrics of your campaign, you are contributing to the mass of e-waste in the cybersphere. Energy company OVO found in 2019 that reducing ONE email per day could result in over 16,000 tonnes of carbon emissions per year. If you are persisting to send emails that aren't being opened, you may be contributing to a hidden carbon monster which is harming our planet. For the sake of our grandkids, make sure that you regularly review the enagement of your contact list and ensure you have a strategy in place for retiring unengaged contacts. As a simple rule, anyone who has not opened your emails in 90 days could be considered unengaged.

Go into the DRAW to WIN

Catchy, right?

As a marketer it is tempting to write compelling subject lines to capture the customers attention and make sure that they are drawn to your emails. But can our creative freedom be causing more harm than good?

The key terms leading to your email demise

WIN, Exclusive, SALE, Deals of the week! - all terms commonly used to portray that we have something exciting for the customer to find within our email. Unfortunately, some email clients have caught on to overly enthusiastic email subject lines and are actively working to reduce the prominence of our email in our customers inbox.

Ensure that even when you have something compelling for your customers that you avoid over use of key promotional terms. By using engaging, brand relevant language and avoiding generic terms (Deal, Sale, etc) not only will your customers be more drawn to your emails, you will bypass automated spam filters which have been proven to deprioritise emails with these terms.

SPF, DKIM, and DMARC

SPF (Sender Policy Framework), DKIM (Domainkeys Identified Mail) and DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting and Conformance) are all email authentication techniques that verify the legitimacy of emails sent and received.

How many acronyms do I need to know to send a simple email!?

Fortunately, you do not need to be innately familiar with these terms and their impact on e-mail delvierability, however, ensuring that your organisations sending domains are properly signed and configured prior to sending your first email are imperative to deliverability.

As part of on-boarding to the Dotdigital platform, our email deliverability consultants will work with your internal IT team to ensure that your email servers are configured for email sending success.

If your domain is not properly configured you will run the risk of emails being rejected by email clients, and a high likelihood that your emails will be flagged as spam or junk. There is also a risk that your emails could be hijacked by malicious operators to steal your customers data.

SMTP messages

SMTP, often thought as Secret Mail Talking Protocol, actually stands for Simple Mail Transfer Protocol. For every email sent and received, your email server and your customers email server perform a secret handshake to determine the success or fail of your email message.

Whilst our email servers are not really having secret conversations behind our back, paying attention to, and acting on the SMTP messages can have a huge affect the deliverability of your emails.

At a high level, the SMTP messages will indicate if the email has succeeded or failed. If we dive into the failures we may be able to identify contacts and patterns to improve our email deliverability.

  1. 4.X.X Codes If you are seeing SMTP messages with 4.X.X codes, this means that there has been a temporary issue in delivering the email to the customers inbox successfully. These issues will range from the customers inbox being full, to the customers email server being offline. Where repeat 4.X.X codes are identified for the same customers, it can be a worthwhile excersize to check with the customer if their details are up to date. Often time, the customer has a new email which, if they provide you, will increase the success of your email deliverability.
  2. 5.X.X Codes If you are seeing SMTP messages with 5.X.X codes, this means that there has been a permanent issue in delivering the email to the customers inbox successfully. These issues will range from the customers email no longer existing, to your email server being blocked by theirs for spammer behaviour. Where 5.X.X issues are encountered, you should verify that you have the correct contact details for your customer on file, and, review your anti-spam practices along with SPF, DKIM and DMARC settings, to ensure that you are not inadvertantly breaching your customers anti-spam measures.

In either case, if you are noticing patterns in your SMTP server messages, it's advised to reach out to your Dotdigital Email deliverability consultant for advice on improving the deliverability of your emails.

Balance - The importance of content types in your emails

Imagine that you are vision-impared. How do you read emails? You don't. Or atleast not in the way that someone with unimpared vision would. Screen readers are a popular instrument for vision impared customers to browse the internet and interact with their computers.

So how does this affect email deliverability?

If you send an email which is entirely made up of images, not only are you discriminating against those with poor eye-sight, but may be inadvertantly losing sender reputation inside your customers email servers. Whilst technologies such as AI are improving this, email servers also have a hard time reading and understanding emails which are entirely made up of images.

A picture tells 1000 words

The perfect marketing image will tell 1000 words about your product without any accompanying text required. But what is it really saying? The easiest way to ensure you are not discriminating and making sure that computers understand your emails is to ensure you include text to compliment the images on your emails. Wherever possible, it's important to include the same message your image is trying to convery in text, either right before or after the image. A few extra characters will go a long way to ensure your emails are being comprehended.

ALT TEXT!

ALT text is an email marketers best friend. For each image in your emails you should ensure to describe the image and what it is trying to convey in the ALT Text field. The best part of ALT text is that it doesn't show up in regular email clients, but those with screen readers will get your message loud and clear, AND email clients will have a clear understanding of what's included in your email.

Review your email strategy today

In conclusion, even with the best intentions, many factors can hinder the successful inbox placement of your email campaigns. By following these Top Five Tips for Successful Email Deliverability, you'll ensure that you are optimizing list hygiene, respecting customer preferences, correctly configuring email servers, avoiding problematic keywords, managing SMTP server messages, and accommodating vision-impaired customers.
